Player Prediction Score (0 – 10)

Every player receives a Predicted Score representing their expected performance in the upcoming match. We compute 7 independent components (each 0–10), combine them with position- and sub-role-specific weights, then apply a sample-size confidence dampening and a venue advantage modifier.

The 7 Components

Form
Season average match rating blended with recent form.
• Outfield: 60% season + 40% last-3-games — rewards current momentum while keeping seasonal stability.
• GK: 50 / 50 — recent clean sheets and blunders are more predictive game-to-game for keepers.
• Fallback: 6.0 if no rating data is available.
Attack
Goal-scoring threat via three independent signals blended 60% season / 40% recent:
• xG per 90 with position-specific scaling (FWD ×14 cap 10 | MID ×14 cap 8 | DEF ×10 cap 5 | GK ×6 cap 3)
• Conversion bonus (0–2): rewards clinical finishers whose goals exceed their xG.
• Shot volume (0–2): shots on target per 90, regardless of xG quality.
Creativity
Fully position-specific:
• DEF — key passes per 90 (build-up quality) + assist delivery bonus. Typical CB ≈ 2; attacking FB ≈ 8.
• MID — xA per 90 (×16, cap 8) + assist bonus + xA-per-key-pass quality + pass accuracy bonus (72%→0, 88%→2).
• FWD — xA per 90 + assist bonus + key pass quality (without the pass accuracy bonus).
Defensive
Position-specific signals:
• GK: save rate (0–7) + goals conceded per game (0–2) + recent save-rate trend (±1.5) + save volume (0–0.5) + pass accuracy (0–1.0).
• DEF: duel win rate (0–4.5) + tackle win rate (0–4.0) + tackle activity (0–1.5) + pass accuracy (0–1.5).
• MID: duel win rate (0–5) + tackle win rate (0–3) + floor of 1.0. DMs score 8+; AMs score ~4.5.
• FWD: hold-up play via duel win rate only (range 1–6).
Availability
Average minutes per game vs a full 90 — detects rotation risk and injury absences.
Blended 50% season / 50% recent. A player averaging 88 min all season but subbed at 45 for 3 games drops from ~8.6 to ~7.2 — a meaningful rotation signal.
Discipline
Starts at 10; each yellow card per game costs 5 points, each red card costs 15.
Blended 40% season + 60% recent — suspension risk is driven by recent behaviour. A player with 3 yellows in 3 games is an immediate concern regardless of a clean season.
Opponent
Player's historical performance against the next opponent specifically. Uses match ratings (and G+A where ratings are missing) across all past meetings.
Formula: 5.0 + (avgRatingVsOpponent − 6.5) × 5.0, clamped 1–10.
Defaults to 5.0 (neutral) when no prior history exists.

Sub-Role Inference

We only store four positions (GK / DEF / MID / FWD) but real footballers play very different roles. The engine infers a sub-role from the player's actual stat signature and adjusts weights accordingly — so a defensive midfielder isn't penalised by the creativity-heavy weights designed for an attacking mid.

Venue Advantage

After the weighted score is computed and confidence-dampened, a venue modifier is applied when the next fixture is known:
Home: +0.25 × confidence  |  Away: −0.20 × confidence
The modifier is scaled by the player's confidence factor so small-sample players aren't over-boosted by venue alone. If no next fixture is scheduled, no modifier is applied.

Sample-Size Confidence Dampening

Per-90 stats become unstable with few games played. We pull the raw score toward a neutral 5.5 for players with limited samples:
Confidence = min(1.0, 0.55 + (gamesPlayed − 3) × 0.0265)

3 games → 55% actual + 45% neutral  |  10 games → 87% actual  |  20+ games → 100% actual (no dampening)

On the player profile page, Prediction Controls scale Form / Threat / Opponent / Minutes / Home-Away and then renormalize. The league grids use default controls.

Risk Levels

Each player is assigned a risk level based on their predicted score. Use this as a guide when deciding who to start.

Low Risk Score ≥ 7.0 Safe to start — strong form, regular minutes, good matchup.
Medium Risk Score 4.5 – 6.9 Proceed with caution — decent pick but some uncertainty.
High Risk Score < 4.5 Consider benching — poor form, rotation risk, or tough fixture.

When viewing All Positions, scores are first normalized within each position group before ranking, so GKs and FWDs compete fairly. The raw score (shown in the card) is restored for display consistency.

Hidden Gems

Hidden Gems are undervalued players whose underlying metrics significantly outpace their visible returns — suggesting untapped potential or a breakout incoming. They must have a predicted score between 4.5 and 8.0 (not too weak, not already elite) and fewer G+A than a position-typical cap (GK: 6 | DEF: 10 | MID: 12 | FWD: 14). Any single one of 7 signals qualifies them:

1
xG+xA outpacing G+A — expected output ≥ 0.25/90 and exceeds actual returns by 40%+. Regression to mean is in their favour.
2
High shot volume, conversion hasn't clicked — ≥ 15 total shots but goals below 22% of shots on target. Consistently in shooting positions; finishing will follow.
3
Creative but unrewarded — Creativity score ≥ 5.0 but assists are below 15% of key passes. Team-mates are spurning the chances, not the player.
4
High threat, few returns — Attack component ≥ 5.0 but G+A total below the position low-return threshold (DEF: 4 | MID: 6 | FWD: 8). Quality not yet visible in the stat line.
5
Premium shot positions — xG per shot ≥ 0.12 across ≥ 6 shots but below the goals threshold. Getting into top-quality positions; conversion is due.
6
Improving trajectory — Recent xG+xA/90 is ≥ 30% above the season average (requires ≥ 3 recent games). Underlying form is genuinely trending up right now.
7
Position-specific specialist quality:
• GK: ≥ 3.0 saves/game + ≥ 1.2 GC/game + ≥ 68% save rate — bailing out a weak defence.
• DEF: ≥ 87% pass accuracy + ≥ 52% duel win rate — elite ball-playing defender not rewarded in G+A.
• MID: ≥ 5 duels/90 + creativity ≥ 4.5 — high-energy engine with creative upside.
• FWD: xG per shot ≥ 0.16 across ≥ 10 shots — consistently occupying premium scoring positions.

Hidden Gems appear separately from Top Picks so the two groups never overlap.

Red Flags

Red Flags highlight players showing alarming declines in form and output. Only players with 270+ minutes overall (or 45+ in recent view) are assessed so new signings aren't unfairly penalised. Ten signals are computed and combined with position-specific weights into a 0–10 alarm score.

The 10 Signals

1
Form Decline — takes the worse of two sub-signals: absolute distance below the 7.0 "good form" baseline ((7.0 − recentRating) / 3.0 × 10) and a relative drop from the season average (each rating point dropped scores 2.5).
2
Output Drop — G+A per 90 compared against position baselines (DEF: 0.10 | MID: 0.22 | FWD: 0.35). Takes the worse of the absolute shortfall and the relative decline from the player's own season average.
3
xG+xA Decline — expected threat per 90 falling vs the season average. An early-warning signal before G+A actually drops.
4
Shot Accuracy Decline — shots-on-target rate falling vs the season average (requires ≥ 10 total shots). Signals a player struggling to test the keeper.
5
Involvement Decline — pass accuracy falling vs the season average (requires ≥ 20 total passes). Fading touch/influence in build-up play.
6
Discipline Risk — recent period only. Each yellow card scores 2.5; each red scores 8.0 (capped at 10). Players on the edge of suspension are surfaced quickly.
7
GK-Specific: Save Rate & Conceding — recent save rate falling vs the season average, plus an additional penalty when conceding ≥ 2.5 goals per game recently.
8
DEF: Duel Win Rate Decline — season vs recent duel win rate comparison (requires ≥ 15 season duels + ≥ 4 recent). Opponents increasingly getting past them.
9
MID: Key Pass Decline — key passes per 90 falling sharply vs season average (requires ≥ 8 total key passes). Creative influence fading — often an early sign of fatigue or loss of role.
10
FWD: xG Per Shot Decline — shot quality (xG per shot) falling vs season average (requires ≥ 10 season shots + ≥ 3 recent). Striker no longer getting into premium positions.

Benchwarmers

Benchwarmers are consistent, reliable players who won't blow you away but won't hurt you either. Great for filling out your squad when premium options aren't available. They must not be Hidden Gems or Red Flags, and need 270+ minutes overall (or 45+ recent).

The 5 Consistency Components

Availability
Average minutes per game vs a full 90. A starter playing every minute scores 10.
Form Consistency
Two sub-signals combined 60/40:
• Band score: how close the season average is to the reliable 6.0–7.5 band (centre 6.75). Volatile highs or lows score low.
• Stability score: how close recent form is to the season average. A player at 6.8 overall but 4.5 recently is not a benchwarmer — they're declining.
Output Reliability
Proximity to a moderate G+A/90 baseline — too far above OR below reduces the score. Benchwarmers are steady, not elite. GKs use save rate proximity instead.
Passing Reliability
Pass accuracy proximity to a position-specific target. Reliable players recycle the ball cleanly without errors or heroics.
Discipline
Card rate per game — reliable players stay on the pitch.
GK: Availability·0.30 + FormConsistency·0.25 + OutputReliability·0.30 + Discipline·0.15
DEF: Availability·0.25 + FormConsistency·0.25 + OutputReliability·0.18 + PassingReliability·0.17 + Discipline·0.15
MID: Availability·0.20 + FormConsistency·0.25 + OutputReliability·0.23 + PassingReliability·0.22 + Discipline·0.10

Synergy

The Synergy tool lets you evaluate a group of players together rather than individually. Picking players from different positions adds a diversity bonus.

Synergy Score = Σ Individual Predicted Scores + (Distinct Positions − 1) × 0.5

A squad covering all four position types (GK, DEF, MID, FWD) earns a +1.5 bonus on top of their combined scores. This rewards balanced squads over stacking one position.
